US FLIGHT DELAYS AND CANCELLATION

When you purchase flight tickets, the airline is responsible for getting you to your destination. However, due to some unforeseen circumstances, flights can be delayed or cancelled.

This visualisation provides information on how much US flights departing from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL) were delayed and how often the flights were cancelled, as well as giving reasons for delays and cancellations for the year 2015.

Note: Information for October 2015 is missing

Departure Delay and Arrival Delay

There are several reasons why flights are delayed. Weather and late aircraft are the 2 major reasons why flights get delayed. Flights are delayed by only few minutes due to Security reasons.

Flights departing from Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL) were delayed more on the day of Christmas Eve (Decemeber 24). On this day, flights departed 38 minutes later than scheduled on average, and arrived 35 minutes later than scheduled, to their destinations. 15 minutes delay was due to late aircraft and 13 minutes delay was due to weather.

Flights departing from ATL are delayed by at least 5 minutes on average. However, it is possible that the delay during arrival (Arrival Delay) is less than the delay during departure (Departure Delay). In Decemeber 2015, flights travelling to Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA) departed 15 minutes late from ATL, but arrived 10 minutes late than the scheduled arrival.

During the month of Christmas, Spirit Airlines (NK) and United Airlines (UA) are the top 2 airlines whose flights are delayed, regardless of whether it is during departure or arrival. This means on the day of Christmas Eve, Spirit Airlines flights and United Airlines flights are more likely to be delayed longer than any other airline's flights.

Spirit Airlines (NK) flights departed 27 minutes late and arrived to their destinations 29 minutes late. Spirit Airlines has been in the top 3 most delayed Airlines since April.

United Airlines was ranked as average in the beginning of the year, but at the end of the year it was ranked the second most delayed airlines.

In December 2013, US Airways (US) had merged with American Airlines (AA). In July 2015, American Airlines had announced its plans to discontinue US Airways by October 2015. This could be the reason why there were no US Airways (US) flights that departed from ATL after June 2015.

Percentage of Flights Cancelled

We saw earlier how common it is for flights to be delayed. It is possible that sometimes flights actually get cancelled.

Although American Eagle Airlines (MQ) was not one of the most delayed airlines all the time, it was the top airline for cancellations in 2015. 8.8% of American Eagle Airlines flights were cancelled. 2.93% of flights were cancelled due to Airline/Carrier, 2.93% cancelled due to National Air System and 2.93% cancelled due to Weather.

Skywest Airlines (OO) had the most cancelled flights in December 2015 (4.79%). 3.59% cancelled due to Weather and 1.20% cancelled due to Airline/Carrier. Therefore, Skywest Airlines flights are more likely to be cancelled during Christmas time.
